The Ferrari F50: An F1 Engine for the Road

A celebratory masterpiece created to mark Ferrari's 50th anniversary, bringing the raw emotion of Formula 1 to the open asphalt.

V12 Displacement 4.7 Liters
RPM Redline 8,500 RPM
0-60 MPH 3.7 Sec
Peak Power 512 BHP

The Carbon Core

At the heart of the F50 lies a revolutionary Cytec aerospace carbon-fiber monocoque. This wasn't just a weight-saving measure; it was a structural manifesto. Unlike its predecessor, the F40, the F50 used the engine as a stressed member of the chassis.

The rear suspension is bolted directly to the engine block, just like the 641 Formula 1 car. This direct connection offers a visceral, unadulterated link between the driver and the machine, transmitting every vibration and symphony from the 4.7L V12 directly into the cockpit.
