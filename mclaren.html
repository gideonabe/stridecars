McLaren F1

Gordon Murray's vision of the ultimate road car. A masterpiece of engineering, featuring a revolutionary carbon-fiber monocoque and a central driving position that remains the gold standard of automotive purity. It wasn't just built to be fast; it was built to be perfect.

Production: 1992—1998
Top Speed 240.1 MPH
0-60 MPH 3.2 SEC
Engine 6.1L V12
Layout 3-SEAT CENTRAL

The Gold Heart

The engine bay is lined with 24kt gold foil—the most efficient heat reflector known to man. This wasn't for ostentation; it was a pure engineering solution to protect the carbon fiber body from the intense heat generated by the BMW-sourced V12 powerhouse.

Engineering Excellence

Carbon Monocoque

The F1 was the first production car to use a complete carbon fiber reinforced plastic (CFRP) monocoque chassis structure. This revolution in materials science provided unprecedented rigidity while keeping the total weight under 1,140kg.
